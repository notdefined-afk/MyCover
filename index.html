My Cover · Loop Health
Loop has built the infrastructure.
My Cover is the layer that makes it usable.
A policy navigator built inside the Loop app — so employees can understand, navigate, and use their insurance before they need it most.
Scroll
Gap
Group health insurance is sold to companies, but used by employees.
The buyer — HR — understands the policy. The employee encounters it for the first time at the worst possible moment: mid-crisis, mid-stress, with no clear path forward.
Understanding a policy requires effort the product rarely makes easy. Employees figure it out when they have to — reaching out to HR insurance PoCs, Googling symptoms alongside coverage questions, or simply making the wrong call and paying for it later.
A self-serve policy intelligence layer changes this for all three parties. Employees get confidence and clarity. HR gets out of the accidental support role they never signed up for. Loop gets better in-network utilisation, cleaner claims, and a stronger renewal story.
What Loop has built
Doctors, insurance, labs, wellness — the full healthcare infrastructure
What's missing
The layer that helps employees understand and activate what they already have
Framework
Three lenses. One product.
My Cover was built by examining the problem from three distinct angles — the structural gap, the buyer's reality, and the user's moment.
Part 1
Gap
Supply is built. Demand activation is missing. A benefit not understood is not a benefit — regardless of how good the underlying product is.
Operational fixes — more doctors, faster claims — don't solve this. It requires a different product surface.
Part 2
Buyer
HR's real job-to-be-done is not administering insurance. It's ensuring the benefit works without becoming the villain in someone's worst moment.
Every failed claim generates an HR ticket. Every HR ticket is a renewal risk. My Cover deflects both.
Part 3
User
The app is opened for the first time under panic. The product was designed for calm, planned interactions. There is a fundamental mismatch between design context and use context.
This navigator is built specifically for high-stress, first-time use moments — not ideal conditions.
Prioritisation
7 anxiety touchpoints. Solved in order of impact.
P2 touchpoints are explicitly deprioritised — either the failure path is recoverable, or Loop already partially solves them through existing channels.
